# **Environment and within-host priority effects jointly predict disease risk in a multi-pathogen system**

This repository contains all data and R code associated with our study of long-term foliar fungal disease dynamics in tall fescue at the Widener Farm in North Carolina, spanning 2017‚Äì2024. We combine random forest models, Bayesian hierarchical models, Cox proportional hazards (survival) analyses, and niche overlap analyses to disentangle the relative roles of environmental conditions and pathogen‚Äìpathogen interactions in driving seasonal disease epidemics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Study Overview

We analyzed more than 41,000 plant-level observations of three co-occurring foliar fungal diseases on tall fescue:

-   **Crown rust** (*Puccinia coronata*)
-   **Anthracnose** (*Colletotrichum cereale*)
-   **Brown patch** (*Rhizoctonia solani*)

Our central question was: how much does a plant's current infection status matter for predicting its future disease risk, relative to local environmental conditions? We find that pathogen‚Äìpathogen interactions (priority effects) can be as important as abiotic drivers, and that for two of our three focal diseases, prior infection by a different pathogen facilitates subsequent infection with the strength of facilitation modulated by environmental context.

## Data

### Disease Observations

Plant-level disease prevalence data were collected across multiple experiments and combined into a single master dataset. Data sources include:

-   Long-term plot surveys at Widener Farm (2021‚Äì2024)
-   Longitudinal surveys from a fenced-plot experiment (2017‚Äì2019), with only untreated/control plots retained
-   Mid-monthly surveys from 2019 and a leaf-level longitudinal dataset from 2018
-   Plot surveys from 2024 (uninoculated, unsprayed control plots only)

For datasets originally collected at the leaf or tiller level, observations were aggregated to the plant level for consistency. Disease presence/absence for each disease was recorded as a binary variable.

### Environmental Data

Daily weather data were obtained from the NC Cardinal network station at the Durham Wastewater Treatment Plant (DURH), which provides continuous records since our monitoring started in 2017. Variables were converted to metric units. A principal components analysis (PCA; 4 components retained) was used to summarize the high-dimensional environmental predictor space. Both raw environmental variables and PC scores were summarized using 7-day rolling averages prior to merging with disease data.

### Key Derived Variables

-   `lag_[disease]`: Disease prevalence in a given plot at the previous survey date
-   `PC1`‚Äì`PC4`: Rolling 7-day average environmental PC scores
-   `env_*`: Rolling 7-day averages of individual weather variables (temperature, humidity, soil moisture, solar radiation, wind speed, etc.)

> **To replicate analyses**, you do not need to re-run `01_DataProcessing.R`. The processed outputs `data/Env+DiseaseData.Rdata` and `data/byleaf2018.Rdata` are provided and are loaded directly by subsequent scripts.

## Scripts

### `01_DataProcessing.R`

Loads and harmonizes raw disease survey data from all field experiments, merges with weather data from the DURH station, computes PCA on standardized environmental variables, calculates 7-day rolling averages for environmental predictors, and generates lagged disease prevalence values. Outputs `data/MasterDataset.csv`, `data/Env+DiseaseData.Rdata`, and a weather loadings heatmap.

**Key packages:** `tidyverse`, `readxl`, `zoo`, `vegan`, `pheatmap`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### `02_RandomForests.R`

Builds temporally explicit random forest classifiers for each of the three focal diseases using `tidymodels`. To prevent data leakage, training/test splits and cross-validation windows are all structured temporally: models are trained on an 80% chronological subset and validated on the remaining 20%, with cross-validation using sliding 12-month training windows predicting 1-month-ahead outcomes. Class imbalance is addressed via upsampling. The `mtry` hyperparameter is tuned across 8 candidate values (4‚Äì32) using parallelized computation.

After model fitting, permutation-based variable importances (via `vip`) and Accumulated Local Effect (ALE) plots (via `iml`) are generated for each disease to identify important predictors and characterize their marginal effects.

**Key packages:** `tidymodels`, `randomForest`, `ranger`, `themis`, `iml`, `vip`, `caret`, `future`

> **Note:** Model fitting takes approximately 30 minutes with 11 cores on MacBook Pro M3 with 18GB RAM. Pre-fitted model objects are saved to `results/` and can be loaded directly (see load statements within the script).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### `03_SurvivalAnalyses.R`

Applies Cox proportional hazards models to the 2018 leaf-level longitudinal dataset. Models are structured with time-varying covariates using start/stop notation, and include environmental PC scores and the lagged presence of the other two pathogens as predictors. Plot ID is used as a clustering variable to account for non-independence. Forest plots of hazard ratios are generated for each disease.

**Key packages:** `survival`, `survminer`, `contsurvplot`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### `04_NicheOverlap.R`

Uses Bayesian niche overlap analysis (`nicheROVER`) to characterize and compare the environmental niches occupied by each disease when present. Niche volumes are estimated in the 4-dimensional environmental PC space using normal-inverse-Wishart posteriors, and pairwise niche overlap probabilities are calculated and visualized.

**Key packages:** `nicheROVER`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### `05_BayesianModels.R`

Fits Bayesian hierarchical logistic regression models (via `brms`/Stan) for each disease, modeling the probability of infection as a function of lagged disease state, month, year, environmental PC scores, co-occurring pathogen presence, pathogen √ó environment interactions, and plot-level random effects. Models include interaction terms between each co-infecting pathogen and all four environmental PCs to test whether the facilitative effect of prior infection is environmentally contingent. Posterior distributions of log-odds are visualized alongside conditional effects plots.

**Key packages:** `brms`, `cmdstanr`, `posterior`, `tidybayes`

> **Note:** Models are run with 6,000 iterations, thinning of 4, and within-chain threading (`threads = threading(2)`). Pre-fitted model objects are saved to `results/` and can be loaded to skip sampling.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### `helper_functions.R`

Utility functions shared across scripts, including `viz_ales_1d()` for generating ALE plots, `gather_posterior()` for extracting and annotating posterior draws from `brms` objects, `stand_classes()` for class balancing, and `tr.ts.by.year()` for year-based train/test splitting.
